Mosaics

The origins of Mosaics

Mosaics originated at around 3000 BCE. They originated in ancient Mesopotamia because people would use small pebbles to create patterns. Mosaics are pieces of art created by placing together small pieces of material such as ceramic, stone, and glass into patterns, or images. A common use for Mosaics are for decorating walls, ceilings, buildings, and floors. A fun fact is that they have been popular ever since the ancient times, And some people use mosaics to depict, or show gratitude to their religion.

How Mosaics are made

Mosaics are made by placing small pieces of material together to create a pattern or image. The pieces are usually made of ceramic, stone, or glass.
